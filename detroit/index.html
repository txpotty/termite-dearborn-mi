Your Pest Management Partner: Recognizing the Signs of Termite Activity

Many homeowners and business owners are unaware they have a termite problem until the damage is significant. Termites are often called 'silent destroyers' because they can work unnoticed for extended periods. Knowing the signs of termite activity is crucial for early detection and intervention, which can save you thousands of dollars in repairs. Don't wait until the damage is extensive to seek professional help. If you notice any of the following signs, it's time to contact Dearborn Termite for a thorough inspection. Early detection is paramount in minimizing the impact of a termite infestation. Being vigilant and knowing what to look for can make a significant difference in the cost and complexity of treatment. We encourage everyone to be aware of these signs and not hesitate to reach out if they have any concerns, no matter how small they may seem.

Common Indicators of Termite Infestation

Here are some of the most common signs that indicate you might have a termite problem:

Discarded Wings

Swarming termites, also known as alates, are reproductive termites that leave their colony to establish new ones. Once they find a suitable location, they shed their wings. Finding small, translucent wings near windowsills, doors, or light fixtures, particularly in the spring or fall, is a strong indicator of a nearby termite colony. These discarded wings are one of the most visible signs of a termite infestation and should never be ignored. The presence of wings suggests a mature colony is present and that new colonies may be forming nearby.

Mud Tubes

Subterranean termites build mud tubes, which are narrow tunnels made of soil, wood, and saliva, to travel between their underground colony and their food source (your home's wood) while protecting themselves from predators and dry air. These tubes are often found on the foundation, walls, or even inside the structure. Mud tubes are definitive evidence of subterranean termite activity and indicate that termites are actively foraging for food. These tubes serve as protected pathways for the termites, allowing them to move freely and continue their destructive work. Inspecting your foundation regularly for these tubes is a simple yet effective way to catch an infestation early.

Damaged Wood

Termites consume wood from the inside out, so the damage may not be immediately visible. However, if you tap on wood and it sounds hollow, or if you notice blistering or sagging wood, it could be a sign of termite damage. As the termite infestation progresses, the structural integrity of the wood will be compromised, leading to noticeable damage. Wood that is soft, easily punctured, or appears to be water damaged in areas without a water source could be indicative of termite activity. Pay close attention to areas where wood meets the soil, as these are common entry points for subterranean termites.

Frass (Termite Droppings)

Termite droppings, also known as frass, resemble small pellets and are often found near termite-infested wood. Drywood termites push their frass out of small holes in the wood. The appearance of frass is a clear sign of a drywood termite infestation and indicates that termites are actively feeding within the wood. While less common than subterranean termites in some areas, drywood termites can still cause significant damage. Identifying frass is key to diagnosing a drywood termite problem.

Bubbling or Peeling Paint

As termites tunnel through wood beneath the surface, they can damage the paint or wallpaper, causing it to bubble, peel, or appear water-damaged. This is because the termites are creating moisture within the wood as they consume it. Don't dismiss bubbling paint as just a moisture issue; it could be a sign of a hidden termite problem. This subtle sign can be easily overlooked, but it's crucial to investigate the underlying cause to rule out termites.

If you observe any of these signs, it is essential to contact a professional termite control service like Dearborn Termite immediately. Early detection and treatment can prevent significant damage and save you considerable time and money in the long run. Ignoring these signs will only allow the infestation to worsen and the damage to your property to increase. Prompt action is the best defense against the destructive power of termites.
